Snakes

Snakes are elongated, limbless reptiles, many of which use venom to hunt their prey.

Interesting Fact

  • The black mamba is the fastest snake, slithering at 12.5 mph.

Snakes

Snakes are fascinating yet dangerous creatures. Here's why:

  • There are over 3,000 species of snakes, but only around 600 are venomous.
  • The inland taipan is the most venomous snake in the world.
  • Snakes can dislocate their jaws to eat prey much larger than their head.
  • They can sense heat through specialized pits near their nose.
  • Snakes shed their skin multiple times a year.
  • The longest snake in the world is the reticulated python, which can reach up to 10 meters.
  • Snakes use their tongues to "smell" their environment.
  • They can go months without eating after a large meal.
  • Snakes have no eyelids; they sleep with their eyes open.
  • Some snake species can "fly" by gliding between trees.
